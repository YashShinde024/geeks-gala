Shadow Commit – Hackathon

Build. Break. Adapt.

Shadow Commit is an intense on-site hackathon organized by the Computer Science Students of K.V. Pendharkar College under Geeks Gala 3.0.

Teams will work against the clock to design, build, and present a solution to a common problem statement. Dynamic in-event elements will be introduced to test adaptability, teamwork, and decision-making skills.

  • Total Duration: 3 Hours
  • Team Size: Max upto 3 participants (Solo, Duo Allowed )
  • Mode: On-site (Computer Lab)
  • Systems: Fixed systems provided (Personal laptops allowed)
  • Total Slots: 15 Teams (First Come, First Served)

Plan of Action

  • Participants report to the lab and occupy their assigned systems and seating.
  • Organizers explain the problem statement, time limits, and general rules.
  • The hackathon officially begins and teams start coding.
  • A special in-event activity or twist is introduced during the hackathon.
  • Teams continue development based on updated instructions.
  • At the end of 3 hours, teams submit their project.
  • Final results are announced after judging.

Rules & Regulations

  • Each team must consist of upto three (3) participants.
  • Teams will be allotted fixed systems and seating, which must not be changed during the event.
  • Teams may bring their own laptops if preferred.
  • The hackathon duration is strictly limited to 3 hours.
  • All teams will receive the same problem statement at the start of the event.
  • Participants must be prepared for dynamic in-event elements that may affect development.
  • Any instructions given by the organizing team must be followed immediately.
  • Disruptive behaviour, arguments, or misconduct will lead to disqualification.
  • Destructive actions such as deleting files, damaging systems, or intentionally breaking projects are strictly prohibited.
  • External help, code copying, or communication with other teams is not allowed.
  • Teams must submit their project before the deadline and be ready for a short live demonstration.
  • Judging Criteria:
    • UI & basic functionality
    • Overall presentation
    • Special judging element (revealed during evaluation)
  • The decisions of the judges and organizing committee are final and binding.
